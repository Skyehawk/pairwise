Setup

Pairwise operates on relative comparisons between teems. You only need to consider the best robot on that alliance, even if everyone playing is only using base drivetrains. There will still be a "Best robot" and a "Worst robot". With this in mind we will develop a rubric on a metric that is difficult to scout with community tools (i.e. driver smoothness). Other metrics such as gamepieces scored are pretty well correlated with stats such as EPA, OPR, etc.

The rubric is designed to mostly be a simple set ofguidelines with room for interpretation an nuance. This needs to be applied evenly across the event, you cannot change what qualifies as meeting a criteria or not part way through. As such we frame things to not be oversensitive to a single occurance of an event and rather on the holistic picture. For example a team shouldn't be overly judged for a single instance of taking a while to line up at a feeder station, but if it happens substantially more than other teams on the alliance that is an issue. By the same token if a team executes a pristine spin move to avoid defense/traffic that is good, but wat we really care about is if they do it consistently and better thatn the other teams on their alliance. Data collection

Overview

Pairwise can analyze data generated from a variety of sources as long as it follows the proper formatting (which can easeily be achieved in a spereadsheet). For the purposes of this tutorial we will use the data generated by the included data generation tool.

The data generation tool creates the following info for each combinaiton of teams on an alliance:
  • Team A
  • Team B
  • How much better Team A is than Team B
  • Match Number
  • If a robot in that comparison was incapacitated for some reason
(More on the specifics of each of these data fields in the other help pages.)

Data is generated in an alliance-by-alliance "tiermaker" format, where the only teams you are considering are the teams currently on the field with blue (or red) bumpers attached. Everything is completly in isolation, as if these are the only 3 robots in the entire world.

There is TBA integration to automatically load the teams that will be playing for that match. You just need to supply the event code and the match number, then press the "request teams" button. The event code can be found by looking at the URL in The Blue Alliance.

    Hardware and Software

    To use Pairwise with as little effort as possible we are going to assume an mobile device connected to cellular internet (not guarenteed in all venues!).

    You will also need a notes app or somewhere to copy/paste data; any simple notes app, a google sheet/doc, etc. will do. At the moment Pairwise does not support data storage.

    Collecting data from Matches

    Let us suppose you find yourself going into an event with two very fast and nimble robots that are likely to be on the first alliance. You find yourself down the order a little bit - competative for sure but there is a very slim chance you are going to beat that likely first alliance paring on pute firepower alone. You decide you stand a chance if you can play the physical game - where you have a bunch of bots tat are not afraid to trade a little paint and land some hits. So let's focus on collecting data on robots that are not afraid to throw their weight around while scoring. From before:

    • Minimal time spent lingering at feeder station
    • "Touch-it-own-it" intake
    • Power through, pushing opponents out of way and gamepieces towards own goal
    • Minimal to no time spent waiting to score, even at (minor) expense of alliance partners
    • Not afraid to bait opponent into drawing a foul
    • Oppertunistic defense to slow opponent down more than it costs themselves in time (i.e. knock opponent gamepiece loose)
    • Hesitent or waiting for opponents to get out of way
    • Avoiding contact or giving up gampiece to opponent bots
    • ovoiding entering opponent zones if there is no risk of foul
    • Gamepieces stuck in robot causing delays in scoring


    With this in mind, we start ranking the teams in each alliance by paying careful attention during eatch match and discussing our observations with a partner. The goal is: no matter how small the differences between teams there should be relativly better teams on alliances and relativly worse teams. In matches where you are playing you need to be honest with yourself if your own team is actually performing better or worse than others, this can be difficult.
